## Source Summary S1 — India digital and social base
Large-scale digital and social adoption in India supports the plausibility of a mass consumer mobile entertainment product. This matters because RANGMANCH is intended to be broad, low-friction, and mobile-native rather than niche or premium-only.

## Source Summary S2 — Spare-time and relationship-driven social usage
Social platforms in India are strongly associated with:
- keeping in touch
- filling spare time
- casual entertainment
These behaviors are directly relevant to RANGMANCH’s intended use moments.

## Source Summary S3 — Screenshot and message-portability behavior
Screenshots are a real way people move content across private digital social spaces. This matters because RANGMANCH’s artifact system is not only aesthetic; it is part of the growth logic.

## Source Summary S4 — Current companion product posture
Many current AI companion products emphasize:
- chat
- memory
- adaptive conversational response
- relationship simulation or emotional engagement
This helps define the category baseline that RANGMANCH must move beyond.

## Source Summary S5 — Emotional-risk evidence in therapy-style AI
Therapy-oriented or emotionally intense AI companion systems can create safety and trust concerns. This supports RANGMANCH’s entertainment-first, emotionally lighter positioning.

## Source Summary S6 — Personalization and relevance
Personalized content tends to become more engaging when it feels specifically relevant to the user. This supports the "my moment / my squad / my life" thesis.

## Source Summary S7 — AI-native memory and continuity
Persistent memory is a key ingredient in making AI products feel more context-aware and relational over time. This supports RANGMANCH’s continuity-rich design instead of one-off novelty generation.

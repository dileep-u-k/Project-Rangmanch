# 13 — Demo Flow Specification

## Purpose of this document
This document defines the exact demo-first flows for Project RANGMANCH.

Its purpose is to specify:
- which flows matter most in the case-study demo
- what sequence of screens should be shown
- what each flow must prove
- and how to maximize clarity and impressiveness during presentation

---

## Why this document matters
A strong prototype can still fail in demo if:
- the first wow is delayed
- the flow is too long
- the best screens are not shown
- or the proof surfaces are buried

Because this is a case-study submission, the prototype must be designed for:
- immediate clarity
- smooth progression
- and strong product proof in a short time

---

# Demo Flow A — First-time wow flow

## Route sequence
Onboarding -> Home -> Episode Setup -> Episode Experience -> Episode Outcome -> Artifact -> Next Act

## What this flow proves
- the app has a differentiated onboarding
- Home clearly communicates the product
- the flagship solo mode is real
- the experience is structured, not chat-generic
- the outcome becomes a portable artifact
- continuity exists

## What should feel strongest
- the onboarding copy
- the episode title/setup
- the choices
- the artifact frame
- the next-act hook

---

# Demo Flow B — Social wedge flow

## Route sequence
Home -> Squad Setup -> Squad Result -> Artifact

## What this flow proves
- the social mode is real
- the product understands group dynamics
- the result is compact and readable
- the output feels screenshot-worthy

## What should feel strongest
- Squad Setup simplicity
- role assignment readability
- verdict clarity
- artifact portability

---

# Demo Flow C — Continuity proof flow

## Route sequence
Home -> Next Act -> Archive

## What this flow proves
- the world does not reset
- continuity is visible
- memory traces exist
- the universe feels alive over time

## What should feel strongest
- Next Act card
- archive traces
- sense of ongoing arcs

---

# Demo sequencing guidance

## Recommended presentation order
1. Demo Flow A
2. Demo Flow B
3. Demo Flow C

This order works because it moves from:
- personal wow
to
- social proof
to
- continuity proof

---

# Demo design rules

## Rule 1
Every demo flow must reach a high-value screen quickly.

## Rule 2
The first 30–60 seconds should already show differentiation.

## Rule 3
Artifact and continuity surfaces should not be skipped in demo.

## Rule 4
Transitions between screens should feel smooth and believable.

## Rule 5
The app should feel like a product, not a prototype maze.

---

# MVP prototype interpretation
The prototype does not need every optional surface to be fully complete before demo.

But these three flows must be:
- stable
- visually coherent
- smooth enough to present confidently

---

## Final summary
The demo-first strategy of RANGMANCH should center on:
- solo wow
- social spread
- continuity proof

If these three flows are strong, the product story becomes much more convincing.

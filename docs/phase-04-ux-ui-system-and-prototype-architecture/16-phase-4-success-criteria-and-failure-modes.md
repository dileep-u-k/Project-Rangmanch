# 16 — Phase 4 Success Criteria and Failure Modes

## Purpose of this document
This document defines:
- how to know whether Phase 4 has actually succeeded
- what failure patterns make Phase 4 weak or incomplete
- what warning signs should be detected before moving to Phase 5

Phase 4 is not complete merely because some screens exist.
It is complete only if the prototype now feels like a coherent product experience rather than abstract product logic.

---

## Phase 4 success criteria

Phase 4 is successful if all of the following are true:

### Success criterion 1 — The app no longer feels abstract
A viewer should now be able to see:
- what the app is
- what the main routes are
- what the wow flow is
- and how the prototype is meant to be used

Required outcome:
The experience is visible and understandable through real screens.

### Success criterion 2 — The wow flow is obvious
The product must clearly expose its strongest MVP path.

Required outcome:
Onboarding -> Home -> Episode -> Outcome -> Artifact -> Next Act feels like an intentional flagship flow.

### Success criterion 3 — The companion feels present but not chat-generic
The interface must preserve companion presence without collapsing into a generic chat experience.

Required outcome:
The companion is visible through banners, narration, commentary, and continuity surfaces.

### Success criterion 4 — The prototype is buildable without ambiguity
Future implementation should not need to guess what the screens or main interactions are.

Required outcome:
The route map, screen specs, and shared widgets make the prototype straightforward to continue building.

### Success criterion 5 — Artifact visuals look compelling
The artifact surface must clearly feel more premium and portable than a normal screen.

Required outcome:
The artifact frame is visually distinct and clearly positioned as the share moment.

### Success criterion 6 — Continuity is visible
The prototype must prove that the world does not reset after each act.

Required outcome:
Next Act and Archive both visibly support continuity and memory.

### Success criterion 7 — The design system feels coherent
The app should feel like one branded product, not a mix of unrelated screens.

Required outcome:
Typography, spacing, cards, motion, and color hierarchy feel aligned.

### Success criterion 8 — The prototype is demo-first
The app should support a smooth presentation path.

Required outcome:
The three demo flows are stable and easy to present.

---

## Practical signs that Phase 4 has succeeded
The builder should now be able to answer clearly:

1. What exact MVP screens exist?
2. What is the primary navigation?
3. What is the first wow flow?
4. How does the companion appear in the interface?
5. What do artifacts look like?
6. What is real vs mocked in the prototype?
7. What exact demo path will be shown?

If these answers are crisp, Phase 4 is working.

---

## Common Phase 4 failure modes

### Failure mode 1 — It still looks like a generic chat app
Symptoms:
- too much plain text
- too much linear dialogue feel
- weak screen differentiation

Why this is dangerous:
The prototype loses originality immediately.

### Failure mode 2 — Too many screens or too much complexity
Symptoms:
- too many routes competing for attention
- weak primary path
- confusing user movement

Why this is dangerous:
The demo becomes messy and the product story weakens.

### Failure mode 3 — Poor home screen clarity
Symptoms:
- unclear primary action
- weak hierarchy
- no visible continuity
- companion presence is thin

Why this is dangerous:
The emotional center of the app fails.

### Failure mode 4 — Weak artifact visuals
Symptoms:
- artifact feels like just another text box
- no premium presentation
- low screenshot-worthiness

Why this is dangerous:
The shareability story becomes weak.

### Failure mode 5 — Overcomplicated onboarding
Symptoms:
- too many inputs
- too much typing
- slow start
- world-entry energy gets lost

Why this is dangerous:
The first wow gets delayed.

### Failure mode 6 — Demo path is not optimized
Symptoms:
- awkward transitions
- too much dead time
- strong screens buried late

Why this is dangerous:
The prototype underperforms in presentation.

### Failure mode 7 — Design inconsistency
Symptoms:
- inconsistent card styles
- inconsistent typography
- uneven spacing
- uneven motion language

Why this is dangerous:
The product feels less credible.

### Failure mode 8 — Companion presence feels thin
Symptoms:
- companion only appears in text labels
- no framing presence
- no identity through screens

Why this is dangerous:
One of the strongest differentiators weakens.

### Failure mode 9 — No clear continuity representation
Symptoms:
- next-act logic is hidden
- archive is meaningless
- the world feels reset after each flow

Why this is dangerous:
The product feels like a one-off novelty.

---

## Warning signs that Phase 4 is still incomplete
Do not proceed if any of the following are true:

- onboarding still feels weak
- home still feels unclear
- episode flow still feels too placeholder-like
- squad flow still feels too placeholder-like
- artifact still feels underdesigned
- continuity still feels invisible
- design system still feels inconsistent
- demo flow still feels awkward
- the product still feels more conceptual than experiential

---

## What success should feel like
When Phase 4 is complete, the prototype should feel:
- branded
- real
- demoable
- structured
- emotionally coherent
- and clearly different from a generic AI wrapper

The goal of Phase 4 is not just more screens.
The goal is product reality.

## Final success statement
Phase 4 is complete only when RANGMANCH is no longer just product-defined and intelligence-defined, but also interface-real, demo-ready, and visually coherent.

# 05 — Home / Universe Screen Spec

## Purpose of this document
This document defines the Home / Universe screen of Project RANGMANCH.

Its purpose is to specify:
- the most important hub surface in the product
- what the user should understand instantly
- what actions should be primary
- and how the world should feel alive

---

## Why Home matters
This is the single most important screen after onboarding.

If Home is weak, the app will feel:
- confusing
- generic
- too tool-like
- or emotionally flat

Home must instantly communicate:
- what RANGMANCH is
- what the main actions are
- that the companion is alive
- and that continuity exists

---

# Home screen required elements

## Element 1 — Companion greeting banner
Purpose:
- establish presence
- create emotional tone
- frame the current moment

Must feel:
- alive
- slightly theatrical
- clear
- product-specific

---

## Element 2 — Today’s Episode primary card
Purpose:
- present the flagship solo mode as the most obvious action

Must communicate:
- personal entertainment
- immediacy
- low-friction entry

---

## Element 3 — Squad Mode secondary card
Purpose:
- present the social wedge clearly

Must communicate:
- group fun
- verdict / drama / chaos energy
- shareable output potential

---

## Element 4 — Mood quick-entry chips
Purpose:
- allow fast low-friction entry
- let the user express state quickly

Recommended moods:
- bored
- chaotic
- dramatic
- chill
- squad energy
- decompression

---

## Element 5 — Active continuation card
Purpose:
- prove the world is alive
- show there is a next act

Must communicate:
- continuity
- active hook
- return-worthy state

---

## Element 6 — Optional archive entry
Purpose:
- hint at memory / continuity depth

This can be lightweight in the MVP.

---

# Layout guidance

Recommended order from top to bottom:
1. Companion greeting banner
2. Today’s Episode primary card
3. Squad Mode card
4. Mood chips
5. Next Act / continuity card
6. Archive entry

This order preserves:
- clarity
- wow
- emotional energy
- and continuity visibility

---

# Interaction guidance
The Home screen should support:
- one-tap move into Episode
- one-tap move into Squad
- chip-triggered mood entry
- one-tap move into continuity

Avoid:
- clutter
- too many parallel CTAs
- weak information hierarchy

---

# Visual guidance
Home should feel:
- like a stage hub
- dynamic but readable
- rich in card hierarchy
- and visually distinct from ordinary utilities

The world must feel:
- in motion
- emotionally expressive
- but still product-clean

---

# Success criteria
A strong Home screen makes the user feel:
- “I understand what this app is”
- “I know what to tap first”
- “this feels alive”
- “this is not a generic AI wrapper”

---

## Final summary
The Home / Universe screen is the emotional center of RANGMANCH.

It should:
- anchor the world
- foreground Episode
- clearly expose Squad
- enable quick mood entry
- and make continuity visible.

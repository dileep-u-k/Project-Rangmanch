# 08 — Success Criteria and Failure Modes

## Purpose of this document
This document defines:
- how to know whether Phase 0 has actually succeeded
- what kinds of failure patterns make Phase 0 incomplete
- what strategic warning signs must be detected before moving to Phase 1

Phase 0 should not be considered finished merely because several documents exist.
It is only complete if the project is strategically clear and stable.

---

## Phase 0 success criteria

Phase 0 is successful if all of the following are true:

### Success criterion 1 — The product can be explained in one sentence
A reader should be able to understand the project from the locked thesis without needing additional interpretation.

Required outcome:
There is one stable thesis statement for RANGMANCH.

### Success criterion 2 — The category is clear
The project must have a precise category identity.

Required outcome:
RANGMANCH is clearly defined as a **Companion-led Personal Entertainment Operating System**.

### Success criterion 3 — The uniqueness logic is clear
The project must not feel like:
- a chatbot clone
- an AI friend clone
- a Character AI style variant
- a roleplay clone
- or a social app with AI garnish

Required outcome:
The originality logic is explicit and defensible.

### Success criterion 4 — Entertainment-first positioning is unambiguous
The project must clearly center:
- delight
- play
- drama
- expressive fun
- social energy

and not drift toward:
- emotional support
- therapy-first framing
- productivity
- utility

Required outcome:
The center of gravity of the product is entertainment.

### Success criterion 5 — The product boundaries are clear
It must be obvious what the product is, what it is not, what is in scope, and what is out of scope.

Required outcome:
The project has a stable identity and anti-identity.

### Success criterion 6 — The central metaphor is locked
The project should have one dominant metaphor that strengthens coherence.

Required outcome:
The metaphor of life as stage and companion as narrator/host/director/chaos engine is accepted and stable.

### Success criterion 7 — The MVP wedge is locked
The first prototype must have a clear proof responsibility.

Required outcome:
The wedge is frozen as:
- Today’s Episode
- Squad Mode
- Shareable Artifact
- Return Hook

### Success criterion 8 — The project principles are stable
There must be a permanent decision framework for later phases.

Required outcome:
The product principles are written and usable.

### Success criterion 9 — All future phases can reference a stable thesis
Research, product definition, AI design, UX, prototype work, and pitch writing should all be able to anchor themselves to Phase 0 outputs.

Required outcome:
Phase 0 acts as a real foundation, not a decorative document set.

---

## Practical signs that Phase 0 has succeeded
The team or builder should now be able to answer clearly:

1. What is RANGMANCH in one sentence?
2. What exact product category is it?
3. Why is it an AI companion product?
4. Why is it not a chatbot clone?
5. Why is it entertainment-first?
6. What is the first strategic wedge?
7. What must the MVP prove?
8. What should the MVP explicitly avoid?
9. What principles should govern later decisions?

If these answers are crisp and stable, Phase 0 is working.

## Common Phase 0 failure modes

### Failure mode 1 — The idea sounds exciting but too broad
Symptoms:
- too many product interpretations
- multiple competing center-of-gravity ideas
- inability to explain the product simply
- early temptation to build many unrelated features

Why this is dangerous:
A broad concept leads to weak MVP scope and poor pitch clarity.

### Failure mode 2 — Confusion between companion and chatbot
Symptoms:
- product described mainly as chat
- open-ended prompt-response logic becoming the default
- inability to explain why the companion is structurally necessary

Why this is dangerous:
The project loses originality and becomes easy to compare with generic LLM products.

### Failure mode 3 — Unclear uniqueness
Symptoms:
- the idea still sounds like an existing product with local flavor
- no strong non-obvious insight
- no clear explanation of the category shift

Why this is dangerous:
The product becomes strategically weak and easy to dismiss.

### Failure mode 4 — Trying to solve too many user problems
Symptoms:
- entertainment, support, productivity, utility, and social networking all mixed together
- no sharp product identity
- feature sprawl appears before design begins

Why this is dangerous:
The product loses focus and becomes impossible to build well in MVP form.

### Failure mode 5 — Accidental drift toward therapy or emotional-support positioning
Symptoms:
- product language starts emphasizing loneliness relief or support-first bonding
- companion behavior starts feeling emotionally heavy
- entertainment starts becoming secondary

### Failure mode 6 — No frozen wedge
Symptoms:
- uncertainty about what the MVP should include
- shifting definitions of the flagship feature
- inability to say what the prototype is actually supposed to prove

Why this is dangerous:
The build phase will become bloated and unstable.

### Failure mode 7 — Principle-free decision making
Symptoms:
- features are proposed without clear logic
- no stable way to judge whether choices fit the product
- debate is driven by taste rather than product philosophy

Why this is dangerous:
Later phases drift and quality declines.

### Failure mode 8 — Metaphor exists only as branding, not as a design tool
Symptoms:
- metaphor is mentioned but does not influence outputs, flows, or UX
- screens feel generic despite strong concept language

Why this is dangerous:
The project loses coherence and memorability.

## Warning signals that Phase 0 is still incomplete
Do not proceed if any of these are true:

- the thesis is still changing frequently
- the category still feels fuzzy
- the product can still be confused with a chatbot
- the entertainment-first positioning is not obvious
- the MVP wedge is not frozen
- multiple incompatible product identities are still being entertained
- the product principles are not being used as real decision tools

---

## What success should feel like
When Phase 0 is truly complete, the product should feel:
- crisp
- non-generic
- category-defined
- strategically grounded
- execution-ready
- and much easier to build than before

The goal of Phase 0 is not more words.
The goal is less confusion.

## Final success statement
Phase 0 is complete only when RANGMANCH is no longer a broad exciting idea, but a thesis-locked, category-defined, wedge-frozen, principle-governed product foundation.

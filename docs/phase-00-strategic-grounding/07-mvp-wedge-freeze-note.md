# 07 — MVP Wedge Freeze Note

## Purpose of this document
This document freezes the MVP of Project RANGMANCH in operational terms.

Its purpose is to prevent scope drift and ensure that the first prototype proves the core category clearly rather than trying to prove the entire long-term platform.

The MVP is not the smallest trivial build.
It is the smallest convincing product slice that proves the category.

---

## Final MVP wedge
The MVP of RANGMANCH is frozen as:

# **Today’s Episode + Squad Mode + Shareable Artifact + Return Hook**

This is the official Phase 0 wedge lock.

---

## Why this wedge is the correct starting point
This wedge is the smallest product slice that can simultaneously prove:

1. Personal life-to-entertainment transformation
2. Social expansion potential
3. Shareability and product portability
4. Continuity and return potential

Any smaller wedge would weaken the concept.
Any broader wedge would risk losing clarity and polish.

---

## What the MVP must prove

### Proof 1 — Life-to-entertainment transformation works
The product must show that a user’s real mood, moment, or situation can be transformed into a structured, entertaining, personalized experience.

Primary proving flow:
- Today’s Episode

### Proof 2 — Solo delight can extend into social energy
The product must show that the experience can expand from private entertainment into socially relevant fun.

Primary proving flow:
- Squad Mode

### Proof 3 — Outputs can travel outside the app
The product must show that results can become portable entertainment artifacts such as cards, verdicts, summaries, or dramatic screens that users would plausibly share or screenshot.

Primary proving surface:
- Shareable Artifact

### Proof 4 — The product can become a recurring system
The product must show that it is not just a novelty generator. It must create a believable reason to come back.

Primary proving surface:
- Return Hook

---

## What the MVP must contain

### Module 1 — Onboarding
The onboarding must:
- be short
- introduce the companion presence
- establish tone
- capture minimal personalization inputs
- move the user quickly into the first wow experience

Recommended onboarding inputs:
- nickname
- vibe/style preference
- humor preference
- optional companion tone preference

The onboarding must not:
- overload the user
- explain too much
- ask for too much data
- feel like account setup rather than world entry

---

### Module 2 — Home / Universe Screen
The home screen must show:
- companion greeting
- Today’s Episode primary CTA
- Squad Mode secondary CTA
- at least one quick mood or vibe entry surface
- one visible continuity or return signal

The home screen should communicate instantly:
**This app turns your current life into entertainment.**

---

### Module 3 — Today’s Episode Flow
This is the flagship MVP flow.

It must include:
- mood or situation input
- companion framing
- episode title
- setup or cast framing
- 2–3 meaningful interaction choices
- outcome
- handoff to artifact and return logic

This flow must prove:
- entertainment-first behavior
- AI-native transformation
- structured outputs
- user agency
- companion orchestration

---

### Module 4 — Squad Mode
This is the core social wedge.

It must include:
- friend names or friend archetype entry
- group entertainment framing
- companion-generated squad output
- outcome or verdict
- handoff to artifact and continuation

This flow must prove:
- social relevance
- group energy
- shareability
- broader product ambition beyond solo use

---

### Module 5 — Shareable Artifact
At least one artifact must exist for:
- Today’s Episode
- Squad Mode

Artifact examples:
- episode card
- dramatic poster
- squad verdict card
- chaos ranking
- next-act teaser card

The artifact must prove:
- social portability
- visual memorability
- brand/product identity
- plausible screenshot/share behavior

---

### Module 6 — Return Hook / Continuity Surface
The MVP must visibly show that the product continues.

Examples:
- Act 2 unlocks tomorrow
- Your squad aftermath is pending
- Your next verdict is waiting
- This season continues
- The next reveal is coming

This can be implemented via:
- a dedicated end-screen
- a Home continuation card
- a Next Act module
- lightweight continuity display

It does not need deep infrastructure, but it must be visible.

---

## Required MVP journeys

### Journey A — First-time solo wow
1. User opens app
2. User completes onboarding
3. User lands on Home
4. User enters Today’s Episode
5. User provides mood or moment
6. Companion frames the episode
7. User chooses a path
8. Outcome appears
9. Artifact appears
10. Return hook appears

This journey proves:
- first clarity
- first delight
- core transformation logic

### Journey B — Social proof journey
1. User opens Squad Mode
2. User enters friend names or archetypes
3. Companion generates squad entertainment output
4. Social result appears
5. Artifact or verdict appears

This journey proves:
- group entertainment logic
- social relevance
- social spread potential

### Journey C — Continuity proof journey
1. User returns to Home after one completed session
2. Product surfaces a pending hook or continuity trace
3. User understands that the system remembers and continues

This journey proves:
- non-randomness
- continuity
- habit potential

## What the MVP must not try to do

### Not required now — Full season engine
The MVP does not need deep long-horizon world building.

### Not required now — Full production memory system
The MVP only needs enough continuity to feel alive.

### Not required now — Full event system
The MVP does not need festivals, watch-party systems, or occasion packs fully implemented.

### Not required now — Full social platform
No feed, follower graph, comments, or community systems are needed.

### Not required now — Monetization implementation
Monetization logic belongs in the strategy layer, not the first build.

### Not required now — Heavy game systems
No points economy, level system, leaderboard backend, or full reward architecture is required.

### Not required now — Rich multimodal stack
A strong text-and-visual artifact prototype is enough.

### Not required now — Advanced analytics infrastructure
Define metrics in strategy docs, but do not overbuild tracking in MVP.

---

## Explicitly excluded from MVP scope
The MVP must exclude:
- open-ended generic chat as primary mode
- too many entertainment modes
- full social network infrastructure
- creator ecosystem
- advice-first or productivity-first modules
- heavy gaming systems
- deep production architecture complexity
- premium or subscription implementation

---

## MVP quality bar

### Quality bar 1 — Concept clarity
A first-time user should understand the product direction quickly.

### Quality bar 2 — Companion centrality
The companion should feel structurally present and important.

### Quality bar 3 — Entertainment quality
The main flow should feel genuinely entertaining, not just AI-generated.

### Quality bar 4 — Social portability
At least one artifact should feel obviously showable or shareable.

### Quality bar 5 — Continuity visibility
The product should clearly signal that it can continue across sessions.

### Quality bar 6 — Scope discipline
The MVP should remain focused and polished rather than broad and messy.

---

## Final lock rule
From this point onward, any proposed MVP feature must justify itself by clearly strengthening one of the four wedge components:
- Today’s Episode
- Squad Mode
- Shareable Artifact
- Return Hook

If it does not, it should be postponed.

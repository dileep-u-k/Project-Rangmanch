# 03 — Quality Framework and Scoring Rubric

## Purpose of this document
This document defines the quality bar for Project RANGMANCH during Phase 7.

Its purpose is to give a consistent evaluation standard for:
- episode outputs
- squad outputs
- artifacts
- hooks
- and full session flows

---

## Core principle
A session should not be judged only on whether it works.

It should be judged on whether it is:
1. clear
2. fast
3. personal
4. entertaining
5. branded
6. interactive
7. portable
8. continuous

That is the Phase 7 quality framework.

---

# Scoring rubric

Use a simple 1–5 scoring scale:

- 1 = weak
- 2 = below bar
- 3 = acceptable
- 4 = strong
- 5 = excellent

---

## Criterion 1 — Clear
Question:
Does the user understand what is happening?

Signals:
- obvious next step
- understandable framing
- low confusion
- readable structure

---

## Criterion 2 — Fast
Question:
Does the payoff arrive quickly enough for mobile attention?

Signals:
- short setup friction
- compact generation
- quick progression to outcome

---

## Criterion 3 — Personal
Question:
Does it feel tied to the user’s mood, moment, or group?

Signals:
- recognizable framing
- relevant cast/setup
- situational specificity

---

## Criterion 4 — Entertaining
Question:
Is there actual delight, amusement, or intrigue?

Signals:
- amusing phrasing
- strong title or verdict
- enjoyable branch choice
- satisfying payoff

---

## Criterion 5 — Branded
Question:
Does it feel like RANGMANCH and not generic generated text?

Signals:
- companion-led tone
- staged entertainment framing
- non-chat-generic presentation

---

## Criterion 6 — Interactive
Question:
Does the user feel they influenced the flow?

Signals:
- meaningful choice
- visible consequence
- active rather than passive feeling

---

## Criterion 7 — Portable
Question:
Does the result naturally support artifact-worthiness or sharing?

Signals:
- strong artifact headline
- compact shareable structure
- emotional readability

---

## Criterion 8 — Continuous
Question:
Does the session connect meaningfully to future use?

Signals:
- specific hook
- visible continuity trace
- believable next step

---

# Overall session quality bar

## Excellent session
Mostly 4s and 5s across the rubric.

## Acceptable session
Mostly 3s with a few 4s.

## Weak session
Multiple 1s or 2s in:
- clear
- entertaining
- portable
- or continuous

Weak sessions should not be used in the main demo story.

---

# Final summary
This rubric should be used across:
- comprehension review
- delight review
- output quality scoring
- shareability review
- and demo-hardening decisions

It is the quality backbone of Phase 7.

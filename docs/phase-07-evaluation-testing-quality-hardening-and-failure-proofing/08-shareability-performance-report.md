# 08 — Shareability Performance Report

## Purpose of this document
This document evaluates whether Project RANGMANCH produces outputs that feel socially portable.

Its purpose is to assess:
- whether users would screenshot or forward outputs
- whether artifacts create curiosity
- which output categories are most socially portable
- and whether the MVP genuinely supports the growth thesis

---

## Core principle
The growth story of RANGMANCH depends heavily on one claim:

that the product can turn personal or social entertainment into compact, portable outputs that feel worth showing.

That claim must be tested.
It should not be assumed.

---

# What shareability testing should ask

The most important shareability questions are:

1. Would a user screenshot this?
2. Would a user forward this to a friend or group?
3. Does the output create curiosity even without full product context?
4. Which format travels best:
   - episode artifact
   - squad artifact
   - verdict card
   - next-act teaser
5. Does the output feel socially safe enough to share?

---

# Current strengths observed

## Strength 1 — Artifact is already the strongest portable surface
The Artifact screen turns the entertainment experience into:
- a framed object
- a compact result
- a clearer emotional takeaway

This is much more portable than a plain generated transcript.

## Strength 2 — Squad outputs are naturally discussable
Squad Mode results are particularly strong because they:
- reference recognizable social roles
- create reaction potential
- and feel like something users may want to show to friends

## Strength 3 — The app does not rely on long-form text for share value
Because the product uses compact, structured outputs, it is much easier to imagine forwarding or screenshotting them.

## Strength 4 — Suggested share caption improves portability
The share-caption layer makes it easier to imagine how the product output could circulate in real usage.

# What makes an output socially portable?

The strongest portable outputs in RANGMANCH tend to be:

- short
- emotionally clear
- socially recognizable
- visually framed
- specific enough to feel personal
- but broad enough to be understandable by others

That is why:
- artifact screens
- squad verdicts
- and compact result cards

are currently the strongest growth-supporting surfaces.

---

# Shareability risks

## Risk 1 — Artifact is visually strong but not emotionally punchy enough
Mitigation:
- strengthen headlines
- tighten verdict language
- improve emotional compression

## Risk 2 — Squad sharing becomes awkward
Mitigation:
- keep humor playful
- avoid humiliation framing
- avoid overly sharp personal attacks

## Risk 3 — Episode outputs are appreciated but not shared
Mitigation:
- strengthen artifact phrasing
- emphasize portable card value
- use stronger “showable result” design language in pitch

## Risk 4 — Shareability is assumed without proof
Mitigation:
- keep this report honest
- present squad artifacts as strongest current proof
- present occasion and teaser sharing as future expansion

# Shareability and growth-story judgment

## Does the MVP support the growth thesis?
Yes, credibly.

But the strongest proof comes from:
- Artifact
- Squad Mode
- and verdict-style compact outcomes

The growth thesis should therefore emphasize:
- artifact sharing
- squad social spread

more than continuity-led sharing in the current MVP.

Continuity is better framed as:
- retention engine first
- growth amplifier second

---

# Recommended improvements

## Improvement 1
Use the strongest artifact screenshots in the deck, especially squad outputs.

## Improvement 2
Sharpen artifact headlines where possible so more outputs feel instantly “worth showing.”

## Improvement 3
Keep next-act teasers positioned as retention logic, not primary current sharing logic.

## Improvement 4
In the final pitch, describe artifacts as:
- portable entertainment objects
- not just generated outputs

---

# Final judgment

## Overall shareability verdict
RANGMANCH already has meaningful portable-output strength, especially through:
- artifact screens
- squad results
- and compact verdict structures

That is enough to support a credible Phase 6 growth story and a strong Phase 7 shareability claim.

# 06 — Demo Script Master

## Purpose of this document
This document defines the controlled live demo script for Project RANGMANCH.

Its purpose is to ensure that the prototype is presented:
- smoothly
- intentionally
- consistently
- and in a founder-grade way

This is not just a click list.
It is a presentation choreography layer.

---

## Core principle
A strong live demo does not try to show everything.

It shows:
- the clearest value
- in the strongest order
- using the strongest screens
- with the least unnecessary friction

For Project RANGMANCH, the demo should prove exactly three things:

1. the product creates a strong first wow  
2. the product broadens socially through Squad Mode  
3. the product supports continuity and return logic  

That is enough.

---

# Demo structure overview

## Sequence A — Onboarding + first wow
Goal:
Show the core product thesis and flagship loop.

## Sequence B — Social proof
Goal:
Show that the product is not only solo entertainment.

## Sequence C — Continuity proof
Goal:
Show that the product is not one-off novelty.

---

# Total demo timing target

Recommended full live demo duration:
**3.5 to 5 minutes**

Suggested split:
- Sequence A: 2.0 to 2.5 minutes
- Sequence B: 1.0 to 1.5 minutes
- Sequence C: 0.5 to 1.0 minute

Do not turn the demo into a long product tour.

---

# Presenter behavior rules

## Rule 1 — Move quickly to Episode
Do not over-spend time on onboarding or Home.

## Rule 2 — Pause longest on:
- Episode Experience
- Artifact
- Squad Result

These are the strongest proof moments.

## Rule 3 — Treat Next Act as strategic proof, not emotional peak
Its job is to prove retention logic, not deliver the largest wow.

## Rule 4 — Use known-good examples
Do not improvise weak live inputs.

## Rule 5 — Do not click everywhere
Only show the strongest path.

## Rule 6 — Keep narration product-grounded
Always connect what is on screen back to:
- adoption
- growth
- retention
- or differentiation

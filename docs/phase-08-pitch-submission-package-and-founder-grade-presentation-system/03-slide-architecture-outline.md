# 03 — Slide Architecture Outline

## Purpose of this document
This document defines the recommended slide structure for the final Project RANGMANCH pitch deck.

Its purpose is to turn the narrative into a disciplined visual sequence.

---

## Core principle
Every slide must earn its place.

The deck should be:
- sharp
- short enough
- visually grounded
- and easy to follow

The goal is not maximum slide count.
The goal is maximum conviction per slide.

---

# Recommended slide sequence

## Slide 1 — Title and one-line thesis
Purpose:
Introduce RANGMANCH in one crisp line.

## Slide 2 — Core user insight
Purpose:
Explain the underlying human / behavioral truth.

## Slide 3 — Problem / market gap
Purpose:
Show why current entertainment and AI options leave a gap.

## Slide 4 — Product definition
Purpose:
Explain what RANGMANCH is.

## Slide 5 — Why now
Purpose:
Explain why the category is timely.

## Slide 6 — Target user / TAM
Purpose:
Show wedge market, broader market, and occasion expansion.

## Slide 7 — Workflow / user journey
Purpose:
Show the flagship end-to-end experience.

## Slide 8 — MVP scope and prototype proof
Purpose:
Show what the MVP includes and why that wedge is enough.

## Slide 9 — Adoption logic
Purpose:
Explain why users try and understand the product quickly.

## Slide 10 — Growth loops
Purpose:
Explain artifact sharing, squad spread, and continuity-assisted growth.

## Slide 11 — Monetization strategy
Purpose:
Explain growth-first, monetization-later.

## Slide 12 — Why this can win / moat
Purpose:
Explain continuity, memory, artifacts, and system-level defensibility.

## Slide 13 — Top risks
Purpose:
Show the main failure classes honestly.

## Slide 14 — Mitigation strategy
Purpose:
Show how those risks are already being handled.

## Slide 15 — Prototype screenshots / demo highlights
Purpose:
Show the strongest visual proof:
- Home
- Episode
- Artifact
- Squad
- Next Act / Archive

## Slide 16 — Closing vision
Purpose:
Leave the reviewer with a category-level conviction statement.

# Optional appendix

# Slide-count quality bar
The deck should feel:
- complete
- but not bloated

A 14–18 slide main deck is the right range.

---

The correct slide architecture moves from:

to
- gap
to
- product
to
- proof

- business logic

# 12 — Phase 8 Exit Gate

## Purpose of this document
This document defines the formal exit gate for Phase 8.

Phase 8 should not be considered complete simply because the deck, APK, and materials have been assembled.
It is complete only if the whole submission works as one coherent Founder’s Office-level package.

---

## Final Phase 8 exit-gate questions

Project RANGMANCH may be considered submission-ready only if all of the following can be answered clearly and consistently.

### Exit Gate Question 1
**Is the pitch story crisp from first slide to last?**

Required answer:
The deck must move cleanly through:
- insight
- problem
- product
- proof
- market
- growth
- monetization
- moat
- risk
- closing conviction

If the deck feels like a pile of information rather than a story, the answer is no.

---

### Exit Gate Question 2
**Does the prototype visibly support the thesis?**

Required answer:
The strongest claims in the deck must map to visible app proof:
- first wow → Episode
- growth → Artifact / Squad
- retention → Home / Next Act / Archive
- differentiation → non-chat-generic structure

If the deck claims more than the app visibly proves, the answer is no.

---

### Exit Gate Question 3
**Does the demo highlight only the strongest moments?**

Required answer:
The demo should focus on:
- Episode Experience
- Artifact
- Squad Result
- continuity proof

It should not wander or attempt to show everything.

---

### Exit Gate Question 4
**Is the risk section mature and credible?**

Required answer:
The final package must honestly name:
- what is still lightweight
- what depends on strong narration
- what future layers deepen later
- and what has already been mitigated

If the package sounds defensive or overconfident, the answer is no.

---

### Exit Gate Question 5
**Are the files professionally packaged?**

Required answer:
The final package should have:
- clean filenames
- clear deliverables
- organized folders
- and obvious reviewer entry points

If the package feels messy or improvised, the answer is no.

---

### Exit Gate Question 6
**Does the whole submission feel Founder’s Office level?**

Required answer:
The product, deck, demo, business case, and package must together feel:
- strategic
- product-grounded
- well-judged
- and high-signal

If any layer feels immature, the answer is no.

---

### Exit Gate Question 7
**Will a reviewer remember this idea after seeing many others?**

Required answer:
The submission should leave a memorable impression around:
- new category feel
- strong wedge
- portable artifact logic
- visible continuity
- and a believable business story

If it blends into generic AI ideas, the answer is no.

## Practical readiness checks

The builder should now be able to:
- explain the product in one line
- explain why it is not just a chatbot
- explain the strongest user loop
- explain the strongest growth loop
- explain the retention proof
- explain the top risks honestly
- walk through the strongest demo path confidently

If any of these are weak, Phase 8 is incomplete.

---

## Hard stop conditions
Do **not** treat submission as final if any of the following are true:

- the deck still feels bloated
- the screenshots still undersell the product
- the demo still has too much variance
- the risk section still feels thin
- the product and pitch still sound slightly misaligned
- the final package still feels messy

---

## Exit gate pass condition
Phase 8 is passed only when:

1. The narrative is crisp  
2. The app visibly supports the story  
3. The strongest demo moments are controlled  
4. The business case is believable  
5. The risk framing is mature  
6. The package is professionally assembled  
7. The idea is memorable  

---

## Final Phase 8 exit-gate statement
Project RANGMANCH may be considered submission-ready only when the pitch, prototype, demo, appendix, and packaging all work together as one coherent founder-grade product proposal.

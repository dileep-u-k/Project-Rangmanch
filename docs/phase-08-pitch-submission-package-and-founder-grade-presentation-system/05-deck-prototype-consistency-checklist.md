# 05 — Deck–Prototype Consistency Checklist

## Purpose of this document
This document ensures that the Project RANGMANCH pitch deck and prototype feel like one coherent system.

Its purpose is to prevent a common failure:
a strong deck describing a product that does not visibly match the app.

---

## Core principle
The deck and prototype should reinforce each other.

The reviewer should feel:
- the story matches the product
- the terms match the UI
- the screenshots match the claims
- and the demo feels like a live version of the same narrative

Nothing should feel disconnected or purely conceptual.

---

# Consistency category 1 — Naming consistency

## Required product terms
Use the same names everywhere:
- RANGMANCH
- Today’s Episode
- Squad Mode
- Artifact
- Next Act
- Archive
- companion
- Home / Universe

## What to avoid
Do not switch terminology casually.

Examples to avoid:
- “Daily Story” instead of “Today’s Episode”
- “Group Mode” instead of “Squad Mode”
- “Share Card” instead of “Artifact”
- “Memory” instead of “Archive” unless explicitly clarifying

Consistency creates trust.

---

# Consistency category 2 — Flow consistency

## The deck should mirror the real demo flow
The deck’s experience explanation should align with:

### Flow A
Onboarding → Home → Today’s Episode → Outcome → Artifact → Next Act

### Flow B
Home → Squad Mode → Squad Result → Artifact

### Flow C
Home → Next Act → Archive → Home reflection

If the deck describes a different product flow than the demo, the submission weakens.

---

# Consistency category 3 — Screenshot consistency

## Screenshots should only come from polished screens
Use only screens that:
- look final enough
- support the key thesis
- are visually clean
- reinforce the story

## Strong screenshot candidates
- onboarding / world entry
- Home
- Episode Experience
- Episode Outcome
- Artifact
- Squad Result
- Next Act
- Archive

## Avoid using
- weak intermediate screens
- visually cluttered moments
- low-clarity states
- unfinished-looking flows

---

# Consistency category 4 — Claim-to-surface consistency

Every major deck claim should point to a real product surface.

## Claim: “The product is not chat-generic”
Proof surfaces:
- Home
- Episode Experience
- Artifact
- Squad Result

## Claim: “Artifacts are the growth surface”
Proof surfaces:
- Artifact screen
- Squad artifact-like outputs
- verdict-style cards

## Claim: “Continuity prevents novelty collapse”
Proof surfaces:
- Home continuity block
- Next Act
- Archive

## Claim: “Squad Mode broadens the product socially”
Proof surfaces:
- Squad setup
- Squad result
- Squad artifact value

---

# Consistency category 5 — Tone consistency

The deck and app should both feel:
- entertainment-first
- playful
- staged
- companion-led
- and strategically coherent

Do not let the deck sound:
- too corporate
- too abstract
- too dry

while the app feels dramatic and alive.

The tone systems should match.

---

# Consistency category 6 — Business-story consistency

The business logic in the deck should map to visible proof in the app.

## Adoption
Maps to:
- fast onboarding
- clear Home
- Today’s Episode first wow

## Growth
Maps to:
- Artifact
- Squad Result
- shareability evaluation

## Retention
Maps to:
- Home reflection
- Next Act
- Archive

## Moat
Maps to:
- continuity
- memory traces
- artifact system
- integrated loop structure

This is critical.

---

# Consistency category 7 — Risk consistency

The deck’s risk section should reflect real Phase 7 findings.

Examples:
- category still benefits from strong narration
- continuity is visible but still lightweight
- weaker ad hoc examples can reduce phrasing impact
- squad phrasing requires safe, playful calibration

Do not invent unrelated risk language just to sound sophisticated.

---

# Final checklist

Before finalizing the deck, confirm:

- product names match app names
- deck flow matches demo flow
- screenshots come from strong screens
- every major claim points to a real surface
- business logic matches what the product visibly proves
- risk language matches actual Phase 7 evaluation
- tone of the deck and tone of the app feel aligned

---

# Final summary
The strongest Phase 8 submission will feel like:
- one product
- one vocabulary
- one experience
- one strategy
- and one coherent founder-grade story

That is the purpose of this consistency checklist.

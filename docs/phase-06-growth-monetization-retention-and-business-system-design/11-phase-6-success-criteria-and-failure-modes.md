# 11 — Phase 6 Success Criteria and Failure Modes

## Purpose of this document
This document defines:
- how to know whether Phase 6 has actually succeeded
- what failure patterns weaken the business case
- what warning signs should be caught before moving to Phase 7

Phase 6 is not complete simply because business notes exist.
It is complete only if those notes form one coherent founder-grade system.

---

## Phase 6 success criteria

### Success criterion 1 — The product can be explained as a real consumer business
The business case should make RANGMANCH sound like:
- a scalable category thesis
- not just a creative prototype

### Success criterion 2 — Adoption logic is sharp
The business case should clearly explain:
- why users try it
- why they understand it quickly
- why the first wow lands

### Success criterion 3 — Retention systems are credible
The product should not rely on one weak return story.
It should show multiple return engines:
- daily curiosity
- continuity
- squad evolution
- occasion relevance
- profile familiarity

### Success criterion 4 — Growth loops are product-native
Growth should clearly come from:
- artifacts
- squads
- continuity
- occasions

not from vague viral assumptions.

### Success criterion 5 — Monetization is sensible and phased
The product should protect:
- wow
- adoption
- sharing
- continuity

before introducing heavier monetization.

### Success criterion 6 — Economics thinking is realistic
The product should show awareness of:
- cost-intensive surfaces
- cost-control logic
- premium subsidy logic

### Success criterion 7 — The moat story is product-based
The product should sound defensible because of:
- continuity
- memory
- social artifact value
- integrated system design
- identity accumulation

not because of generic AI claims.

### Success criterion 8 — The metrics framework is actionable
The metrics should measure:
- activation
- engagement
- retention
- growth
- monetization-readiness

through the actual product loops.

---

## Practical signs that Phase 6 has succeeded
You should now be able to answer clearly:

1. Who adopts this first?
2. Why do they return?
3. How does the product spread?
4. What stays free and what becomes premium later?
5. Why could this product win over time?
6. What would prove the business is working?

If those answers are crisp, Phase 6 is strong.

---

## Common failure modes in Phase 6

### Failure mode 1 — Vague TAM
Symptoms:
- giant broad market claims
- no wedge
- no user behavior framing

Why this is dangerous:
The market story sounds inflated and unserious.

### Failure mode 2 — Hand-wavy growth
Symptoms:
- vague claims that “people will share”
- no specific product surfaces
- no product-native loops

Why this is dangerous:
The growth story becomes weak immediately.

### Failure mode 3 — Weak retention explanation
Symptoms:
- only one generic return reason
- no continuity logic
- no layered return system

Why this is dangerous:
The product still sounds like novelty.

### Failure mode 4 — Monetization too early or too aggressive
Symptoms:
- paywalling wow
- harming artifacts
- harming adoption

Why this is dangerous:
The business story feels immature and growth-hostile.

### Failure mode 5 — No economics realism
Symptoms:
- no discussion of cost-heavy surfaces
- no structure-aware efficiency thinking

Why this is dangerous:
The product sounds strategically shallow.

### Failure mode 6 — Moat framed only as “AI”
Symptoms:
- overemphasis on models or prompts
- weak product accumulation story

Why this is dangerous:
The defensibility story becomes generic and weak.

### Failure mode 7 — Metrics too broad or irrelevant
Symptoms:
- vanity metrics
- too many metrics
- no tie to product loops

Why this is dangerous:
The business case feels ungrounded.

---

## Warning signs that Phase 6 is still incomplete
Do not move ahead if:
- the wedge market still feels fuzzy
- adoption still sounds generic
- retention still sounds like habit language instead of product logic
- growth loops still sound magical
- monetization is still too aggressive
- moat still sounds mostly technical
- metrics still do not reflect visible product behavior

---

## Final summary
Phase 6 is complete only when the business story of RANGMANCH feels:
- specific
- behavior-grounded
- product-native
- operationally realistic
- and founder-grade

That is the bar.

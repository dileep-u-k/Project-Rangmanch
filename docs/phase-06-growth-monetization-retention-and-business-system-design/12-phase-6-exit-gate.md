# 12 — Phase 6 Exit Gate

## Purpose of this document
This document defines the formal exit gate for Phase 6.

Phase 6 should not be considered complete simply because business documents exist.
It is complete only if those documents create one believable, product-native, founder-grade business system around RANGMANCH.

---

## Final Phase 6 exit-gate questions

Project RANGMANCH may proceed beyond Phase 6 only if all of the following can be answered clearly and consistently.

### Exit Gate Question 1
**Why do users adopt after first exposure?**

Required answer:
The product must have a clear adoption story built on:
- immediate payoff
- self-relevance
- novelty with structure
- emotional lightness
- social value
- continuity curiosity

It should not rely on vague AI novelty.

---

### Exit Gate Question 2
**What makes users return beyond novelty?**

Required answer:
The product must show multiple retention engines:
- daily episode curiosity
- arc continuation
- squad evolution
- occasion relevance
- personalized familiarity

At least the first three must already be meaningfully present in MVP logic.

---

### Exit Gate Question 3
**What specific product-native growth loops exist?**

Required answer:
The business case must clearly identify:
- artifact sharing loop
- squad invitation loop
- continuity curiosity loop
- occasion relevance loop

The first two must be presented as the strongest MVP-visible loops.

---

### Exit Gate Question 4
**What stays free, and what monetizes later?**

Required answer:
The product must protect:
- first wow
- basic artifact value
- basic continuity proof
- early shareability

Premium layers should be clearly phased and respectful of adoption.

---

### Exit Gate Question 5
**Why is the business potentially defensible?**

Required answer:
The moat must be product-based and cumulative:
- continuity
- memory
- social artifacts
- integrated loop design
- identity accumulation

It must not be framed mainly as “AI.”

---

### Exit Gate Question 6
**What metrics would prove early success?**

Required answer:
The metrics framework must clearly define:
- activation
- engagement
- retention
- growth
- monetization-readiness

And those metrics must map to visible product surfaces and loops.

---

### Exit Gate Question 7
**Why does this feel like a real business, not just a concept?**

Required answer:
The business system must sound:
- behavior-grounded
- strategically coherent
- operationally realistic
- and directly tied to the actual MVP

## Practical readiness checks

The builder should now be able to:
- explain who the product is for
- explain why the first wedge is strong
- explain why users return
- explain how artifacts support growth
- explain why monetization is delayed
- explain why the moat compounds over time
- explain what metrics matter first

If these cannot be explained in a sharp way, Phase 6 is incomplete.

---

## Hard stop conditions
Do **not** move into Phase 7 if any of the following are true:

- TAM still feels inflated or vague
- adoption sounds generic
- retention logic sounds weak
- growth loops sound magical
- monetization feels too early
- moat still sounds mostly technical
- metrics do not map to real product behavior

---

## Exit gate pass condition
Phase 6 is passed only when:

1. The business story is specific  
2. The product loops support that story  
3. The growth and retention logic are believable  
4. Monetization is phased sensibly  
5. The moat is cumulative and product-based  
6. Early success metrics are actionable  
7. The case sounds founder-grade  

---

## Final Phase 6 exit-gate statement
Project RANGMANCH may proceed beyond Phase 6 only when the MVP is surrounded by a credible business system that feels realistic, scalable, and directly grounded in the product experience.

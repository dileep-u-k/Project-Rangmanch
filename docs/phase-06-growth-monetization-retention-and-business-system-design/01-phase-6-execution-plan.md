# 01 — Phase 6 Execution Plan

## Purpose of this document
This document defines the correct execution order for the business-system work of Phase 6.

Its purpose is to ensure that the business case is built in a coherent sequence.

## Core rule
Do not treat Phase 6 as eight separate essays.

Instead, build it as one connected system in this order:
1. business thesis
2. market framing
3. adoption
4. retention
5. growth loops
6. monetization
7. economics
8. moat
9. metrics

## Correct execution order

### Step 1 — Lock the business thesis
Create a concise statement of:
- what business RANGMANCH is in
- what category it is creating
- what its wedge is
- and why it matters

### Step 2 — Define the market properly
Frame TAM through user behavior and situations, not inflated abstract numbers.

### Step 3 — Explain adoption
Describe why users try it and why the first wow happens.

### Step 4 — Explain retention
Describe why users return after novelty.

### Step 5 — Explain growth loops
Describe how the product spreads through artifacts, squads, occasions, and continuity.

### Step 6 — Explain monetization
Define what remains free and what becomes premium later.

### Step 7 — Add economics realism
Explain generation cost awareness and product cost controls.

### Step 8 — Define defensibility
Explain why the product can accumulate advantage over time.

### Step 9 — Define metrics
Explain what early success would look like.

## Execution quality bar
Every document should be:
- specific
- product-grounded
- connected to real surfaces in the app
- directly reusable in the pitch deck

## Final rule
If a business statement cannot be tied back to:
- a user behavior
- a product surface
- a product loop
- or an observable metric

then it is too vague and should be rewritten.

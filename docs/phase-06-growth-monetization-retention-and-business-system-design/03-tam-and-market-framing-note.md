# 03 — TAM and Market Framing Note

## Purpose of this document
This document defines the market framing of Project RANGMANCH.

Its purpose is to show:
- who the initial wedge market is
- what the broader market looks like
- what situational expansion layers exist
- and why the category can scale

---

## Core principle
RANGMANCH should not use a hand-wavy TAM story.

The market should be framed as a behavior-driven opportunity stack.

That means:
- who uses it first
- what behavior makes them likely adopters
- where expansion happens
- and why this category can become broad over time

---

# Layer 1 — Initial wedge market

## Definition
The initial wedge market is:

mobile-first young users with high short-session entertainment frequency and strong social expressiveness.

## Best-fit user groups
This includes:
- college students
- hostel-based friend groups
- highly online campus users
- early-career friend circles
- users who already exchange memes, banter, screenshots, and lightweight group content
- users comfortable trying new entertainment formats if payoff is quick

## Why this wedge is strong
This wedge is ideal because these users already display:
- high mobile frequency
- boredom-response behavior
- high screenshot/share behavior
- high group-context humor
- strong appetite for novelty if it is low-friction

## Why this wedge fits the MVP
The MVP proves exactly the surfaces this wedge responds to:
- Today’s Episode
- Squad Mode
- Artifact portability
- continuity curiosity

---

# Layer 2 — Broader entertainment-seeking market

## Definition
The broader market is:

users seeking low-friction, lightweight, personalized entertainment during idle or social moments.

## Broader behavioral profile
This includes users who:
- want something more active than passive scroll entertainment
- enjoy content that feels about them or their group
- enjoy playful personalization
- are open to repeatable AI experiences as long as they are simple and fun
- do not want heavy emotional AI experiences

## Why this market matters
This is where RANGMANCH expands beyond the initial socially expressive wedge into a wider consumer entertainment category.

---

# Layer 3 — Occasion-driven expansion market

## Definition
The occasion-driven expansion layer is:

high-energy moments where entertainment frequency and sharing intent spike naturally.

## Priority occasions
This includes:
- birthdays
- festivals
- sports nights
- watch parties
- trips
- group meetups
- campus events
- weekends
- celebration spikes
- boredom spikes

## Why occasion expansion matters
Occasions amplify:
- session intent
- emotional energy
- artifact shareability
- group participation
- and install curiosity

This makes occasion-driven use an important expansion surface.

---

# Category framing

## What category is RANGMANCH creating?
RANGMANCH is best framed as:

a companion-led personal entertainment operating system

This category sits between:
- AI companions
- social fun products
- short-session mobile entertainment
- and personalized interactive content

## Why this category can scale
The category can scale because it is powered by four broad consumer forces:
1. mobile-first idle-time entertainment
2. desire for self-relevant content
3. social sharing of lightweight humor and identity signals
4. increasing comfort with AI as an experience layer

---

# Wedge-to-expansion ladder

## Start
Daily solo and social entertainment moments.

## Expand
Repeat usage, squad participation, artifact sharing.

## Widen
Occasion-driven entertainment and broader playful use cases.

## Deepen
Continuity, identity, and premium layers over time.

This ladder is the correct market expansion logic.

---

# Final summary
RANGMANCH should frame its market not as one giant entertainment number, but as a layered opportunity:
- wedge market first
- broader behavior market second
- occasion expansion third

This makes the business case feel realistic, behavioral, and product-native.

# 10 — Metrics Framework

## Purpose of this document
This document defines what early success would look like for Project RANGMANCH.

Its purpose is to show:
- which metrics matter first
- how product behavior maps to measurement
- what signals would validate adoption, retention, growth, and monetization readiness
- and how to avoid vanity metrics

---

## Core principle
The metrics framework should reflect the actual product loops of RANGMANCH.

That means the most important metrics should map to:
- onboarding
- Today’s Episode usage
- Squad Mode usage
- artifact creation and portability
- continuity return behavior
- and premium demand signals later

The goal is not to measure everything.
The goal is to measure the few things that reveal whether the product is truly working.

---

# Metric layer 1 — Activation metrics

## Why activation matters
Activation tells us whether users understand the product and reach its first real value moment.

For RANGMANCH, activation should mean:
- the user gets through onboarding
- experiences the core flow
- and reaches a meaningful entertainment payoff

## Core activation metrics

### Metric 1 — Onboarding completion rate
Definition:
percentage of users who complete onboarding after opening the app

Why it matters:
shows whether the entry flow is clear and low-friction

### Metric 2 — First episode completion rate
Definition:
percentage of users who start Today’s Episode and reach the outcome

Why it matters:
shows whether the flagship loop is strong enough to carry a user to payoff

### Metric 3 — First artifact creation/view rate
Definition:
percentage of users who reach the artifact surface after a completed flow

Why it matters:
artifact visibility is central to both growth and product understanding

### Metric 4 — Time to first wow
Definition:
median time from app entry to first meaningful entertainment payoff

Why it matters:
RANGMANCH depends on fast delight

---

# Metric layer 2 — Engagement metrics

## Why engagement matters
Engagement shows whether users interact beyond one shallow pass.

## Core engagement metrics

### Metric 1 — Episode sessions per active user
Definition:
average number of Today’s Episode sessions per active user

Why it matters:
shows whether the flagship loop is repeatable

### Metric 2 — Squad Mode usage rate
Definition:
percentage of active users who use Squad Mode at least once

Why it matters:
shows whether the social wedge is relevant, not just decorative

### Metric 3 — Artifact generation / view rate
Definition:
average number of artifact screens reached per active user

Why it matters:
artifact usage is tied directly to growth logic

### Metric 4 — Branch completion rate
Definition:
percentage of episode sessions where users pick a path and reach the outcome

Why it matters:
shows whether the interactive structure is clear and satisfying

### Metric 5 — Archive revisit rate
Definition:
percentage of users who return to Archive after at least one completed act

Why it matters:
shows whether continuity surfaces matter

---

# Metric layer 3 — Retention metrics

## Why retention matters
Retention is the most important proof that the product is more than novelty.

## Core retention metrics

### Metric 1 — D1 retention
Definition:
percentage of users who return the next day

Why it matters:
basic first signal of repeat value

### Metric 2 — D7 retention
Definition:
percentage of users who return within 7 days

Why it matters:
stronger signal of repeatable entertainment value

### Metric 3 — Repeat episode rate
Definition:
percentage of users who complete more than one episode across separate sessions

Why it matters:
shows whether the flagship loop actually repeats

### Metric 4 — Continuity return rate
Definition:
percentage of users who revisit Next Act or Archive after a completed flow

Why it matters:
proves whether the continuity layer has behavioral pull

### Metric 5 — Multi-mode return rate
Definition:
percentage of users who use both solo and social flows over time

Why it matters:
shows whether the product broadens naturally once users understand it

---

# Metric layer 4 — Growth metrics

## Why growth metrics matter
Growth metrics reveal whether the product has low-CAC expansion potential.

## Core growth metrics

### Metric 1 — Artifact share rate
Definition:
percentage of active users who use share or save behavior on artifacts

Why it matters:
artifact-sharing is the primary MVP growth loop

### Metric 2 — Invite or curiosity conversion proxy
Definition:
percentage of social sessions that plausibly create external curiosity or re-entry behavior

Why it matters:
Squad Mode should help organic discovery

### Metric 3 — Organic install share
Definition:
share of new users arriving through non-paid channels

Why it matters:
indicates product-native acquisition potential

### Metric 4 — Squad participation depth
Definition:
frequency and recurrence of social-mode usage

Why it matters:
the stronger social usage becomes, the stronger group-led growth can become

---

# Metric layer 5 — Monetization-readiness metrics

## Why monetization-readiness matters
Even before monetization is implemented, the product can generate signals that show where premium value may exist.

## Core monetization-readiness metrics

### Metric 1 — Premium-style mode demand
Definition:
interest in richer dramatic modes, premium presentation, or deeper continuity

Why it matters:
indicates which premium surfaces feel naturally valuable

### Metric 2 — Save rate for premium-feeling artifacts
Definition:
which artifact styles or outputs users value enough to keep

Why it matters:
signals demand for premium presentation layers

### Metric 3 — Willingness-to-pay indicators
Definition:
qualitative or behavioral signs that users want more customization, depth, or intensity

Why it matters:
helps determine monetization sequencing

---

# Metrics hierarchy

## Most important early metrics
1. onboarding completion
2. first episode completion
3. first artifact view
4. D1 retention
5. repeat episode rate
6. continuity return rate
7. artifact share rate

These are the clearest early signals.

## Important but secondary early metrics
- Squad Mode usage rate
- archive revisit rate
- D7 retention
- monetization-readiness indicators

---

# What the pitch should emphasize
The deck should highlight a small number of meaningful early metrics rather than many weak ones.

Best emphasis:
- activation
- repeat usage
- artifact share rate
- continuity return rate

This makes the business story sharper.

---

# Metrics risks

## Risk 1 — Measuring vanity metrics
Mitigation:
focus on metrics tied to core product loops

## Risk 2 — Too many metrics too early
Mitigation:
prioritize the few that reveal adoption, retention, and growth truth

## Risk 3 — Metrics disconnected from product surfaces
Mitigation:
tie every important metric to a visible app behavior

---

# Final summary
The strongest early metrics for RANGMANCH are the ones that prove:
- users understand the product
- users complete the core loop
- users return
- users create portable outputs
- and users show signs of sharing or wanting more depth

That is the correct Phase 6 metrics framework.

# 15 — Business Risks and Assumptions Note

## Purpose of this document
This document identifies the key assumptions and business risks inside the Phase 6 case.

Its purpose is to make the Phase 6 story mature, honest, and defensible.

---

## Core assumptions

### Assumption 1 — Users want active lightweight entertainment
The business case assumes that a meaningful segment of users wants something more interactive than passive scrolling, but lighter than deep emotional AI.

### Assumption 2 — Self-relevant entertainment is sticky
The business case assumes users will value content that reflects:
- their mood
- their moment
- their group
- and their identity

### Assumption 3 — Portable outputs improve growth
The business case assumes artifact-style outputs are more likely to travel socially than long chat transcripts.

### Assumption 4 — Continuity improves return behavior
The business case assumes even lightweight continuity can create higher repeat usage than one-off novelty alone.

### Assumption 5 — Social group framing broadens usage
The business case assumes Squad Mode can expand the product from solo entertainment into socially alive usage.

---

## Key business risks

### Risk 1 — The category remains too niche
If users see the product as only a novelty, it may not broaden into a repeatable consumer category.

Mitigation:
- strengthen continuity
- strengthen artifact value
- sharpen first wow and repeat wow

### Risk 2 — Artifacts are not shareable enough
If artifacts do not feel worth sending, the primary growth loop weakens.

Mitigation:
- improve visual polish
- tighten artifact copy
- improve emotional punch of headlines and verdicts

### Risk 3 — Retention remains shallow
If continuity feels weak or artificial, the product may underperform after first use.

Mitigation:
- strengthen hook specificity
- deepen archive meaning
- improve profile familiarity

### Risk 4 — Social mode feels risky or awkward
If Squad Mode feels mean, weak, or generic, the social loop may not spread safely.

Mitigation:
- keep humor playful
- keep role structures compact
- avoid socially sharp or humiliating framing

### Risk 5 — Monetization is introduced too early
If premium gates weaken wow or sharing, adoption could suffer.

Mitigation:
- keep monetization phased
- delay aggressive premium logic
- protect core free value

### Risk 6 — Defensibility remains too shallow
If continuity, memory, and artifacts do not deepen over time, the product may be copied at the surface level.

Mitigation:
- strengthen cumulative identity systems over time
- focus on integrated product loops rather than isolated features

---

## Final summary
The strongest Phase 6 business case is not one that ignores risk.
It is one that names the real assumptions, exposes the main risks, and shows a credible mitigation path.

That is how the project sounds founder-grade rather than naive.

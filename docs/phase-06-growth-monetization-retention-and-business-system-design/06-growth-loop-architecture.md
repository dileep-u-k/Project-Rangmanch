# 06 — Growth Loop Architecture

## Purpose of this document
This document defines how Project RANGMANCH spreads through product-native loops.

Its purpose is to show:
- how the product can grow with low CAC
- why sharing is credible
- what surfaces naturally invite other users in
- and how retention and growth reinforce each other

---

## Core principle
Growth should not be explained through vague statements like:
- “this could go viral”
- “people will obviously share it”
- or “AI products spread naturally”

Instead, growth should come from specific loops that are already visible in the product.

RANGMANCH should grow through:
- artifact sharing
- squad invitation behavior
- occasion relevance
- continuity curiosity

---

# Growth Loop A — Artifact sharing loop

## Flow
1. user experiences delight
2. result becomes an artifact
3. user shares artifact
4. recipient becomes curious
5. recipient tries the product
6. recipient creates their own artifact

## Why this is the strongest primary loop
The artifact is the cleanest portable value object in the product.

It works because it is:
- compact
- emotionally legible
- self-relevant
- socially portable
- and visually framed

## Strongest product surfaces
- artifact screen
- episode outcome
- squad result
- suggested share caption

## Strongest artifact candidates
- episode verdict card
- dramatic outcome card
- squad ranking card
- “portable chaos summary” card
- next-act teaser card in future versions

## Loop strengths
- low friction
- easy to explain
- strong fit with screenshot behavior
- native to the current MVP

## Loop risks
- artifacts may not always feel strong enough to share
- users may consume privately without forwarding
- some artifact text may need stronger emotional punch

## Strengthening opportunities
- improve visual styles
- improve caption wording
- create slightly more distinct artifact variants
- sharpen headlines and share hooks

---

# Growth Loop B — Squad invitation loop

## Flow
1. user generates a squad result
2. output references friends or group archetypes
3. user wants reaction from the group
4. friends are shown the result or invited in
5. group curiosity rises
6. more group usage occurs

## Why this matters
This is the strongest social expansion loop because it enters existing group behavior.

Users already:
- joke about friends
- forward screenshots
- discuss social verdicts
- share lightweight group humor

## Strongest product surfaces
- squad setup
- squad result
- artifact after squad
- future shared or replay flows

## Loop strengths
- highly discussable
- socially native
- easy to demonstrate in pitch
- broadens the product beyond solo usage

## Loop risks
- humor can become awkward if too sharp
- social output may feel weak if not specific enough
- loop depends on artifact or verdict quality

## Strengthening opportunities
- keep outputs playful rather than harsh
- emphasize recognizability
- improve social verdict compactness
- later add replay or reaction variants

---

# Growth Loop C — Occasion relevance loop

## Flow
1. a special moment occurs
2. RANGMANCH feels timely
3. user creates an occasion-linked experience or artifact
4. the result feels more shareable because it is timely
5. installs or re-entries rise
6. event memory reinforces brand

## Why this matters
Occasions increase:
- emotional energy
- entertainment demand
- sharing intent
- and relevance

That makes them powerful growth accelerators.

## Strongest occasion categories
- birthdays
- festivals
- sports nights
- watch parties
- trips
- campus events
- weekends

## Strongest product surfaces
- future occasion cards
- future event mode entry points
- themed artifact styles
- occasion-specific copy

## Loop strengths
- high contextual relevance
- strong spike potential
- good fit for social groups

## Loop risks
- not fully built in MVP
- can sound too future-heavy if overemphasized
- requires strong occasion design later

## Strengthening opportunities
- mention as expansion logic, not current main proof
- keep only top-priority occasions in deck
- tie it clearly to current artifact logic

---

# Growth Loop D — Continuity curiosity loop

## Flow
1. user gets an unresolved or evolving hook
2. the hook drives return
3. the next session creates a new output
4. that output creates new artifact potential
5. new artifacts create new share opportunities

## Why this matters
This is not a direct viral loop, but an indirect growth amplifier.

Retention increases:
- session count
- output count
- artifact count
- and therefore share opportunities

## Strongest product surfaces
- next act
- archive
- home continuity card
- remembered traces

## Loop strengths
- product-native
- improves repeat usage
- makes growth less dependent on first-use novelty

## Loop risks
- continuity may feel shallow in MVP
- hooks may feel generic if not tied to real traces

## Strengthening opportunities
- keep hooks specific
- improve archive relevance
- deepen episode-to-hook linkage over time

---

# Loop hierarchy

## Strongest current MVP growth proof
1. Artifact sharing loop
2. Squad invitation loop

## Strong but still lighter in MVP
3. Continuity curiosity loop

## Most future-facing
4. Occasion relevance loop

This hierarchy is important for the final pitch.
Do not present all loops as equally mature.

---

# Why the growth story is credible
The growth system is credible because it is rooted in product surfaces that already exist:
- artifacts
- squad results
- continuity
- and short-session outputs

That makes the story stronger than generic consumer AI virality claims.

---

# Final summary
RANGMANCH should present four growth loops:
- artifact sharing
- squad invitation
- occasion relevance
- continuity curiosity

But for the MVP and submission, the clearest proven loops are:
- artifact portability
- squad social spread
- and continuity-assisted re-entry

That is the correct founder-grade growth framing.

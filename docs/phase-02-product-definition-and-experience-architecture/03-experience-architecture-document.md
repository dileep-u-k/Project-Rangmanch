# 03 — Experience Architecture Document

## Purpose of this document
This document defines the complete experience architecture of Project RANGMANCH.

Its job is to explain:
- how the product is structured conceptually
- what the major surfaces are
- what the top-level modes are
- what the session architecture is
- how users move through the product
- how the companion fits into the experience
- and how continuity and portability are embedded into the product

This is the main Phase 2 architecture deliverable.

---

## 1. Experience architecture principle
RANGMANCH should not feel like:
- a blank AI tool
- a generic chatbot
- a passive content feed
- or a utility-first app

It should feel like:
- a living entertainment universe
- a companion-led experience system
- a stage where life becomes structured entertainment

This means the architecture must reinforce:
- companion presence
- product clarity
- entertainment framing
- session momentum
- social portability
- continuity

---

## 2. Top-level product surfaces

### Surface 1 — Onboarding
The entry experience where the user is introduced to the companion and lightly personalizes the world.

### Surface 2 — Home / Universe
The central hub where the user sees:
- companion greeting
- Today’s Episode
- Squad Mode
- quick-entry surfaces
- continuity signals

### Surface 3 — Today’s Episode flow
The flagship solo entertainment flow.

### Surface 4 — Squad Mode flow
The flagship group/social entertainment flow.

### Surface 5 — Artifact / Share surface
The output surface where private delight becomes portable.

### Surface 6 — Continuity / Next Act surface
The return layer that proves the product is alive beyond one interaction.

### Surface 7 — Archive / Memory surface
A non-core-but-valuable layer for history, recap, and continuity depth.

### Surface 8 — Profile / Customization surface
A lower-priority surface for tone and personalization control.

---

## 3. Core experience loop
The core RANGMANCH loop is:

**entry -> context -> framing -> interaction -> payoff -> artifact -> return hook**

This is the defining experience structure of the product.

---

## 4. Product architecture layers

### Layer A — Entry layer
This includes:
- onboarding
- home
- quick mood entry
- mode selection

Its job is to reduce friction and create clarity.

### Layer B — Context layer
This includes:
- mood input
- situation input
- social context input
- vibe and tone signals

Its job is to let the system understand what kind of moment this is.

### Layer C — Framing layer
This is where the companion interprets the moment and transforms it into:
- an episode
- a squad scene
- a verdict
- a mini-drama
- or another structured entertainment object

### Layer D — Interaction layer
This is where the user:
- chooses
- reacts
- branches
- moves through the framed experience

### Layer E — Payoff layer
This is where the product delivers:
- the outcome
- the dramatic resolution
- the emotional/comic payoff

### Layer F — Portability layer
This is where the output becomes:
- a card
- a poster
- a verdict artifact
- a summary worth showing

### Layer G — Continuity layer
This is where the product signals:
- next act
- active hook
- remembered world
- continuity beyond now

---

## 5. What makes the architecture coherent
The product becomes coherent when:
- the companion is visible across surfaces
- all major flows share the same loop logic
- outputs feel like entertainment objects
- social portability is built into the architecture
- continuity exists at the end of the main loop
- the MVP surfaces all reinforce the same product story

---

## 6. MVP-active experience surfaces

### Required MVP-active surfaces
- onboarding
- home / universe
- Today’s Episode
- Squad Mode
- artifact / share
- continuity / return hook

### Conceptually mapped but not required in MVP
- archive / memory
- profile / customization
- future live moment or challenge surfaces

---

## 7. Companion role inside the architecture
The companion should be present as:
- greeter at entry
- framer in the main flows
- host between surfaces
- voice behind the outcomes
- source of next-act continuity

The companion should not disappear into backend invisibility.

---

## 8. Architectural rule for later phases
Every major experience surface should answer one of these:
- How do I enter the world?
- How does my moment become entertainment?
- How do I interact with that entertainment?
- What do I get at the end?
- What do I show others?
- Why do I come back?

If a surface answers none of these well, it should be reconsidered.

---

## Final architecture summary
RANGMANCH’s experience architecture is built around:
- a clear entry
- context capture
- companion-led framing
- structured interaction
- emotional payoff
- artifact portability
- and continuity-rich return logic

That is the skeleton the prototype and later UX must follow.

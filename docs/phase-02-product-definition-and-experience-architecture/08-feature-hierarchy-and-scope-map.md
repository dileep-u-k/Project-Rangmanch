# 08 — Feature Hierarchy and Scope Map

## Purpose of this document
This document defines the feature hierarchy of Project RANGMANCH.

Its purpose is to answer:
- which features are absolutely core
- which features are valuable but secondary
- which features are optional if time allows
- which features are explicitly post-MVP
- and how the product avoids feature sprawl

This is a scope-discipline document, not a brainstorming document.

---

## Why this document matters
RANGMANCH is a naturally expansive concept.
If every interesting idea is treated as equally important, the product will become:
- bloated
- unclear
- under-polished
- hard to demo
- and strategically weak

The purpose of this hierarchy is to protect:
- clarity
- build quality
- MVP coherence
- and proof strength

---

## Scope hierarchy principle
All features should be judged by one question:

**Does this feature directly strengthen the core proof of RANGMANCH as a companion-led personal entertainment operating system?**

If the answer is yes, it is probably core or near-core.
If not, it is likely optional or post-MVP.

---

# Tier 1 — Core submission-defining features

These are the features without which the MVP would fail to prove the category.

## Feature 1 — Onboarding
### Purpose
Introduce the world, companion, and lightweight personalization.

### Why it is core
Without onboarding, the product loses:
- world entry
- companion framing
- first personalization
- emotional setup

### MVP status
Required

---

## Feature 2 — Home / Universe screen
### Purpose
Act as the main product hub.

### Why it is core
Without Home, the product loses:
- clarity of product direction
- central navigation
- visible companion presence
- continuity signal
- top-mode coherence

### MVP status
Required

---

## Feature 3 — Today’s Episode flow
### Purpose
Transform the user’s mood or moment into an interactive entertainment experience.

### Why it is core
This is the flagship product proof.
Without it, the product loses:
- life-to-entertainment transformation
- structured solo experience
- companion-led framing
- first wow

### MVP status
Required

---

## Feature 4 — Squad Mode flow
### Purpose
Turn friend-group dynamics into socially meaningful entertainment.

### Why it is core
Without Squad Mode, the product loses:
- social wedge
- group identity relevance
- growth plausibility
- socially alive positioning

### MVP status
Required

---

## Feature 5 — Artifact / Share surface
### Purpose
Turn outcomes into compact, portable entertainment objects.

### Why it is core
Without artifacts, the product loses:
- screenshot-worthiness
- product portability
- social spread plausibility
- stronger emotional payoff

### MVP status
Required

---

## Feature 6 — Return Hook / Continuity signal
### Purpose
Show that the world continues beyond one session.

### Why it is core
Without return logic, the product risks feeling like:
- a one-off novelty generator
- a cool demo with no retention logic

### MVP status
Required

---

## Feature 7 — Companion greeting and framing
### Purpose
Keep the companion visibly central across the experience.

### Why it is core
Without this, the product becomes:
- tool-like
- generic
- less category-distinctive

### MVP status
Required

# Tier 2 — Strong secondary features

These features strengthen the product meaningfully, but the MVP can still work without fully building them.

## Feature 8 — Quick mood / vibe chips on Home
### Purpose
Reduce friction for fast entry.

### Why it matters
Supports short-session delight and rapid activation.

### MVP status
Strongly recommended

---

## Feature 9 — Archive / Memory screen
### Purpose
Allow the user to revisit prior episodes, artifacts, or continuity traces.

### Why it matters
Strengthens continuity and memory perception.

### MVP status
Optional if time allows

---

## Feature 10 — Profile / Companion customization
### Purpose
Let users tune some aspects of tone or presentation.

### Why it matters
Can strengthen ownership and personalization.

### MVP status
Optional if time allows

---

## Feature 11 — Home continuity module
### Purpose
Show active arcs or “next act” signals directly on Home.

### Why it matters
Makes the world feel alive without requiring a separate continuity mode.

### MVP status
Strongly recommended
Can be part of Home rather than separate

# Tier 4 — Post-MVP features

These are valuable but should not be fully implemented for the submission MVP.

## Feature 15 — Full Scene This mode
### Why post-MVP
Interesting but not required to prove the core wedge.

## Feature 16 — Full Challenge Me mode
### Why post-MVP
Good future retention layer, but not required now.

## Feature 17 — Full What Next? continuity mode
### Why post-MVP
Retention depth can begin with lightweight hooks rather than a full mode.

## Feature 18 — Full Live Moment Mode
### Why post-MVP
Occasion amplification is powerful, but everyday product strength comes first.

## Feature 19 — Full archive engine
### Why post-MVP
Useful but not necessary to prove continuity conceptually.

## Feature 20 — Deep companion customization system
### Why post-MVP
Can wait until the core experience is strong.

## Feature 21 — Full event-specific packs or modes
### Why post-MVP
Occasion sensitivity should amplify the product later, not define MVP scope.

---

# Tier 5 — Explicitly excluded from submission MVP

These are not “later in this same build.”
These are intentionally excluded from the case-study prototype.

## Exclusion 1 — Full social graph
No follower system, contact graph, or in-app social network.

## Exclusion 2 — Public feed system
No browse-heavy content feed or creator feed.

## Exclusion 3 — Advanced monetization implementation
No subscriptions, purchases, or payment flow implementation.

## Exclusion 4 — Production-grade moderation stack
No deep moderation infrastructure required for submission prototype.

## Exclusion 5 — Complex analytics infrastructure
No advanced dashboards or event pipelines needed now.

## Exclusion 6 — Voice-first interaction
Not necessary for MVP proof.

## Exclusion 7 — Rich media generation stack
Not necessary for MVP proof.

## Exclusion 8 — Heavy game systems
No points economy, rank ladder, reward architecture, or progression economy.

## Exclusion 9 — Productivity / assistant features
No drift toward planning, scheduling, or advice-first utility.

---

# Scope-discipline rules

## Rule 1
The MVP must optimize for proof strength, not feature count.

## Rule 2
Every Tier 1 feature must map to one of the core wedge proofs:
- transformation
- social expansion
- portability
- continuity

## Rule 3
Tier 2 features may be added only if Tier 1 feels strong and stable.

## Rule 4
Tier 3 teaser features should never steal execution time from Tier 1 quality.

## Rule 5
Tier 4 and Tier 5 items should not leak into MVP through uncontrolled scope creep.

---

## Final summary
The feature hierarchy of RANGMANCH is now frozen conceptually.

This means the product has:
- a clear core
- a clear near-core
- a clear future layer
- and a clear exclusion boundary

That is essential for building a strong submission-grade MVP.

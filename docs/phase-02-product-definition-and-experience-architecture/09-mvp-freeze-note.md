# 09 — MVP Freeze Note

## Purpose of this document
This document formally freezes the submission MVP of Project RANGMANCH.

Its purpose is to define:
- exactly what will be built
- exactly what proof the MVP must deliver
- exactly what is optional
- exactly what is excluded
- and exactly what later phases should treat as fixed

This is the final Phase 2 MVP lock document.

---

## Why the MVP freeze matters
Without a formal freeze, the project can drift into:
- mode sprawl
- weak demo focus
- unfinished features
- poor polish
- unclear product story

A strong MVP is not the one with the most features.
It is the one that proves the product most convincingly.

---

# Final MVP definition

## The submission MVP of RANGMANCH is frozen as:

### Core build set
- Onboarding
- Home / Universe
- Today’s Episode flow
- Squad Mode flow
- Share Artifact surface
- Return Hook / Continuity signal

This is the official MVP lock.

---

# What the MVP must prove

The MVP must prove the following four truths clearly:

## Proof 1 — Personal transformation
The product can take a real mood, moment, or situation and turn it into a personalized entertainment experience.

Primary proving flow:
- Today’s Episode

---

## Proof 2 — Social expansion
The product can move from solo delight into friend-group relevance and social energy.

Primary proving flow:
- Squad Mode

---

## Proof 3 — Portable product value
The output can become a compact artifact worth showing, saving, or reacting to.

Primary proving surface:
- Artifact / Share

---

## Proof 4 — Continuity and return potential
The product is not a one-off novelty machine.
It can signal an evolving world and a reason to come back.

Primary proving surface:
- Return Hook / Continuity signal

---

# Exact MVP modules

## Module 1 — Onboarding
### Must include
- brief world entry
- nickname or identity cue
- vibe or style cue
- humor or tone preference
- companion introduction

### Must achieve
- immediate category signal
- light personalization
- low friction

### Must avoid
- long setup
- too many inputs
- utility-like form behavior

---

## Module 2 — Home / Universe
### Must include
- companion greeting
- Today’s Episode primary CTA
- Squad Mode secondary CTA
- quick mood or vibe chip entry
- visible continuity signal

### Must achieve
- immediate product clarity
- visible world feeling
- obvious next action

### Must avoid
- dashboard clutter
- blank-tool feel
- weak companion presence

---

## Module 3 — Today’s Episode flow
### Must include
- moment or mood capture
- companion framing
- structured episode setup
- 2–3 meaningful choices
- outcome
- transition to artifact and return hook

### Must achieve
- first-session wow
- life-to-entertainment proof
- strong structured entertainment

### Must avoid
- generic chat sprawl
- overly long setup
- weak payoff

---

## Module 4 — Squad Mode flow
### Must include
- friend names or archetypes
- group setup
- companion-led social framing
- structured group output
- verdict/result
- transition to artifact

### Must achieve
- social proof
- group recognizability
- “this is our squad” feeling

### Must avoid
- too much data entry
- weak legibility
- generic group humor with no structure

---

## Module 5 — Artifact / Share surface
### Must include
- at least one artifact style for solo output
- at least one artifact style for squad output
- compact, legible, emotionally clear display

### Must achieve
- screenshot-worthiness
- portability
- product identity
- social plausibility

### Must avoid
- looking like raw text pasted onto a card
- weak emotional clarity
- visual confusion

---

## Module 6 — Return Hook / Continuity signal
### Must include
- next-act line
- or continuation card
- or active aftermath / pending hook
- and ideally a trace on Home

### Must achieve
- sense of aliveness
- reason to return
- world continuity

### Must avoid
- generic re-engagement language
- meaningless “come back later” messaging
- no actual continuity signal

---

# MVP quality bar

The MVP is acceptable only if it demonstrates:

## Quality bar 1 — Product clarity
A first-time user understands what the product is.

## Quality bar 2 — Companion centrality
The companion feels essential and visible.

## Quality bar 3 — Entertainment strength
The core flow is genuinely entertaining.

## Quality bar 4 — Social legibility
Squad Mode feels socially meaningful and recognizable.

## Quality bar 5 — Artifact quality
At least one artifact feels worth showing.

## Quality bar 6 — Continuity visibility
The world clearly signals ongoing life.

## Quality bar 7 — Scope discipline
The product feels focused, not overloaded.

The MVP build commitment is now:

## Build now
- Onboarding
- Home / Universe
- Today’s Episode
- Squad Mode
- Artifact / Share
- Return Hook / Continuity

## Build later only if time remains
- Archive
- Profile / style
- teaser surfaces

## Do not build now
- everything else outside this scope

# Final lock rule
From this point onward, every proposed addition to the MVP must answer:

**Does this make the core proof stronger enough to justify its implementation cost?**

If not, it should be postponed.

# 07 — User Journey Maps

## Purpose of this document
This document defines the main user journeys of Project RANGMANCH.

Its purpose is to make the product concretely understandable by showing:
- what the user wants
- what screens they move through
- what the companion does
- what the product outputs
- and what emotional payoff the user gets

This is one of the most important Phase 2 documents because it translates product theory into user-visible experience.

---

## Why user journeys matter
A product can have:
- good modes
- good surfaces
- good features

and still fail if the experience journey is vague.

User journeys prevent that by forcing clarity around:
- entry
- progression
- transition
- outcome
- and return logic

For RANGMANCH, the journeys must show how the product actually feels in use.

---

# Journey 1 — First-time user journey

## Journey purpose
This is the most important journey in the MVP.
It proves:
- product clarity
- first-session wow
- companion presence
- entertainment transformation
- artifact value
- and return logic

---

## User intent
The user wants:
- something new
- something fun
- something fast
- something more personal than generic content

They do not yet know the system deeply.

---

## Screen sequence
1. Splash / Entry
2. Onboarding Screen 1
3. Onboarding Screen 2
4. Home / Universe
5. Today’s Episode Setup
6. Episode Experience
7. Episode Outcome
8. Artifact / Share
9. Return Hook / Continuation

---

## Step-by-step journey

### Step 1 — Splash / Entry
The user opens the app.

#### AI / companion behavior
Minimal but clear world-entry presence.

#### Output type
Atmosphere and entry cue.

#### Emotional payoff
Curiosity.

---

### Step 2 — Onboarding Screen 1
The user enters a nickname or identity cue and selects a vibe.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion lightly welcomes the user into the world.

#### Output type
Personalized setup tone.

#### Emotional payoff
Early sense of recognition.

---

### Step 3 — Onboarding Screen 2
The user selects a humor style or companion flavor.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion signals that it will shape the experience accordingly.

#### Output type
Tone calibration.

#### Emotional payoff
The world starts to feel tailored.

---

### Step 4 — Home / Universe
The user lands on the main hub.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion greets the user and frames the world as active.

#### Output type
Greeting, main mode options, quick-entry cues.

#### Emotional payoff
Clarity:
“I understand what this app wants me to do.”

---

### Step 5 — Today’s Episode Setup
The user enters a current mood or moment.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion interprets and prepares the framing.

#### Output type
Context input.

#### Emotional payoff
Expectation:
“This is about my moment.”

---

### Step 6 — Episode Experience
The system reveals the episode framing and presents choices.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion narrates, frames, and guides.

#### Output type
Title, setup, cast logic, choices.

#### Emotional payoff
Delight and engagement.

---

### Step 7 — Episode Outcome
The user sees the result of the episode.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion closes the scene and reveals the payoff.

#### Output type
Outcome, summary, verdict-like resolution.

#### Emotional payoff
Recognition, amusement, surprise, or satisfaction.

---

### Step 8 — Artifact / Share
The result becomes a portable entertainment object.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion presents it as something worth keeping or showing.

#### Output type
Card, poster, verdict, or dramatic result artifact.

#### Emotional payoff
“That is showable.”

---

### Step 9 — Return Hook / Continuation
The system signals that this is part of a continuing world.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion teases what comes next.

#### Output type
Next-act line, pending aftermath, continuation cue.

#### Emotional payoff
Anticipation.

---

## First-time journey success condition
At the end of this journey, the user should believe:
- this is not a generic chatbot
- this app can turn my moment into entertainment
- this app can produce something worth showing
- this app feels alive enough to revisit

---

# Journey 2 — Repeat solo journey

## Journey purpose
This journey shows how the product becomes repeatable for individual use.

---

## User intent
The user wants:
- another entertaining moment
- something fresh
- a return to the companion world
- continuity or variety depending on the state

---

## Screen sequence
1. Home / Universe
2. Quick Entry or Today’s Episode Setup
3. Episode Experience
4. Episode Outcome
5. Artifact / Share
6. Return Hook / Home continuity

---

## Step-by-step journey

### Step 1 — Home / Universe re-entry
The user opens the app again.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion greets with familiarity and may reference active continuity.

#### Output type
Greeting + continuity signal + primary CTA.

#### Emotional payoff
Familiarity.

---

### Step 2 — Quick Entry or Today’s Episode Setup
The user either taps a quick mood chip or enters a new situation.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion interprets whether this should feel fresh, familiar, escalating, or different.

#### Output type
Context input or quick entry.

#### Emotional payoff
Low-friction re-entry.

---

### Step 3 — Episode Experience
The user enters a new but still RANGMANCH-consistent episode.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion keeps tone continuity while ensuring freshness.

#### Output type
Framed episode, choices, interaction.

#### Emotional payoff
Novelty with familiarity.

---

### Step 4 — Episode Outcome
The user gets the emotional payoff.

#### AI / companion behavior
Companion reveals outcome and reacts to the user’s world.

#### Output type
Outcome / summary / payoff.

#### Emotional payoff
Repeat delight.

---

### Step 5 — Artifact / Share
The result becomes optionally portable.

#### AI / companion behavior
Companion offers a showable interpretation.

#### Output type
Artifact.

#### Emotional payoff
Memory, shareability, identity reinforcement.

---

### Step 6 — Return Hook or Home continuity update
The system updates the world and signals continuation.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion preserves the feeling of world continuity.

#### Output type
Updated hook / continuity trace.

#### Emotional payoff
“This product remembers and continues.”

---

## Repeat solo journey success condition
At the end of this journey, the user should feel:
- the product still feels fresh
- the product still feels like itself
- the companion feels familiar
- and the world did not reset into randomness

---

# Journey 3 — Squad / social journey

## Journey purpose
This journey proves social expansion, group identity relevance, and artifact-driven sharing potential.

---

## User intent
The user wants:
- to interpret group dynamics
- to make the group laugh
- to label chaos
- to create something to show friends
- or to react to something that just happened

---

## Screen sequence
1. Home / Universe
2. Squad Setup
3. Squad Result
4. Artifact / Share
5. Continuation line or Home return

---

## Step-by-step journey

### Step 1 — Enter Squad Mode from Home
The user chooses the social path.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion signals that this mode is about cast dynamics and group chaos.

#### Output type
Mode framing.

#### Emotional payoff
Expectation of group fun.

---

### Step 2 — Squad Setup
The user adds friends or archetypes and lightly defines the context.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion prepares the group frame.

#### Output type
Social context input.

#### Emotional payoff
Recognition:
“This is about our group.”

---

### Step 3 — Squad Result
The product generates the group-specific output.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion acts as host/director/judge/chaos engine.

#### Output type
Roles, verdicts, rankings, mini-dramas, labels, or squad scenes.

#### Emotional payoff
Laughter, recognition, social accuracy, reaction potential.

---

### Step 4 — Artifact / Share
The group result becomes a card or result worth showing.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion frames the result as a showable object.

#### Output type
Squad artifact.

#### Emotional payoff
“This is worth sending.”

---

### Step 5 — Continuation line or Home return
The product lightly signals the world continues.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion suggests:
- aftermath
- future verdict
- recurring squad energy
- or next act

#### Output type
Short continuity line.

#### Emotional payoff
The squad world feels alive.

---

## Squad journey success condition
At the end of this journey, the user should feel:
- this product understands group energy
- this is more than solo entertainment
- this could spread socially
- and the result is legible enough to show others

---

# Journey 4 — Continuity / return journey

## Journey purpose
This journey proves that RANGMANCH can feel like a living system rather than a novelty generator.

---

## User intent
The user wants:
- to see what continues
- to revisit a previous thread
- to feel that the product remembers
- or simply to check what’s next

---

## Screen sequence
1. Home / Universe
2. Continuity / Home hook module
3. Optional continuation entry
4. New or continued micro-experience
5. New hook

---

## Step-by-step journey

### Step 1 — Home / Universe re-entry
The user opens the app again after previous use.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion signals that the world has not reset.

#### Output type
Greeting + active hook or callback.

#### Emotional payoff
Continuity recognition.

---

### Step 2 — Continuity / Home hook module
The user sees:
- next act
- active aftermath
- prior trace
- or an invitation to continue

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion positions the world as progressing.

#### Output type
Hook card / continuation module.

#### Emotional payoff
Curiosity.

---

### Step 3 — Optional continuation entry
The user chooses whether to continue or start something fresh.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion adapts to either:
- progression
or
- fresh entry

#### Output type
Branch into continued or new flow.

#### Emotional payoff
Agency plus continuity.

---

### Step 4 — New or continued micro-experience
The user gets a compact interaction that reflects prior context or current state.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion preserves continuity without making the product feel heavy.

#### Output type
Continuation beat, updated episode fragment, aftermath, or fresh act.

#### Emotional payoff
The world feels alive.

---

### Step 5 — New hook
The product ends with a renewed continuity signal.

#### AI / companion behavior
The companion keeps the user’s entertainment universe in motion.

#### Output type
Next hook.

#### Emotional payoff
Return plausibility.

---

## Continuity journey success condition
At the end of this journey, the user should feel:
- the product remembers
- the world continues
- the product is not just a one-off machine
- I could come back again for the next act

---

# Journey coherence rules

## Rule 1
Every major journey must end in a clear payoff.

## Rule 2
Every major journey should either include or naturally lead to an artifact.

## Rule 3
Every major journey should include a visible companion presence.

## Rule 4
Every major journey should make the product feel more like a world and less like a tool.

## Rule 5
At least one strong return cue should exist by the end of every core journey.

---

## Final summary
The four main journeys of RANGMANCH together prove:
- what the product is
- why it feels different
- how it spreads
- and why it can become sticky

These journeys are now the core user-experience logic that later UI, AI, and engineering work must implement faithfully.
